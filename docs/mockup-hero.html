How to choose a WooCommerce hosting plan in 2026
⋮⋮
Why hosting matters more than you think
⋮⋮
When you pick a host for a WooCommerce store you're really picking three things at once. You're picking where the database lives, where PHP runs every time someone hits the cart, and where the static assets for your theme sit on disk. Most stores never outgrow shared hosting — but the ones that do hit a wall so hard they lose a holiday season to it, so it's worth thinking about this before you launch.
⋮⋮
Vitalink · Improve
In this guide I'll walk through the four tiers — shared, managed WordPress, VPS, and dedicated — and tell you exactly when each one stops being worth the money. I'll also share the three concrete questions I ask every client before recommending a host.
⋮⋮
📷   hero-image.jpg · 1600×900
⋮⋮
The TL;DR: most stores under 500 orders/day should be on managed WordPress hosting. Below that you're paying for capacity you don't use. Above that, you need at least a VPS with Redis object cache — and you need it before Black Friday, not after.
